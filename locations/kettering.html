Why Kettering Is Different

Concrete challenges specific to Kettering, Ohio

Kettering sits just south of Dayton in Montgomery County, and most of its residential neighborhoods were built between the 1950s and 1970s. That means the original concrete driveways, sidewalks, and patios in areas like Lincoln Park, Oakwood-adjacent Kettering, Van Buren, and Rosewood are 50 to 70 years old -well past the 25-30 year service life of a typical residential slab.

The soil beneath Kettering is predominantly heavy Brookston silty clay loam, which expands when wet and contracts when dry. This seasonal movement is the number-one cause of cracked and settled driveways we see on streets like Wilmington Pike, Dorothy Lane, and Stroop Road. Many homeowners have tried patching, but once the base has failed under an aging slab, the only lasting fix is removal, proper base compaction, and a new pour.

Kettering also has a strong culture of home improvement. The city's code enforcement is active, mature tree canopies lift sidewalk panels, and homeowners in neighborhoods near Delco Park and Indian Riffle Park take pride in curb appeal. Driveway and patio replacement is the most common project we do in Kettering, followed closely by sidewalk panel replacement where tree roots have caused trip hazards.

Because many Kettering lots are compact with narrow side yards, we frequently use smaller equipment and careful staging to protect lawns and landscaping. Our crews know the city's right-of-way rules and permit process, and we handle all of that for you.
