Why Centerville Is Different

Concrete work in Centerville and Washington Township

Centerville, located in Washington Township south of Dayton, is one of the most desirable residential communities in the Miami Valley. Neighborhoods like Yankee Trace, Rahn Estates, Normandy Ridge, and Washington Village feature well-maintained homes where curb appeal and outdoor living spaces are a priority. That makes Centerville our top market for stamped concrete and decorative patio work.

The soil profile here is similar to the rest of southern Montgomery County -heavy clay with moderate drainage -but many Centerville subdivisions were graded more carefully during development, which means fewer catastrophic base failures compared to older neighborhoods in Dayton proper. The main concrete issues we see in Centerville tend to be cosmetic aging, surface spalling from de-icer use, and outdated plain-finish slabs that homeowners want to upgrade to stamped or exposed-aggregate finishes.

Centerville homeowners often request color-matched or design-coordinated concrete that ties into existing hardscaping, stone veneers, and landscaping. Our crews carry sample boards on every estimate visit so you can see stamp patterns, integral colors, and release agent combinations in person, against your actual home exterior. Popular patterns in Centerville include ashlar slate, random fieldstone, and herringbone brick.

For driveways, the wider lots common in subdivisions along Clyo Road and McEwen Road often accommodate three-car-wide aprons and flared entries. We engineer every driveway to handle the actual load -including the delivery trucks, landscaping trailers, and dumpsters that larger properties regularly see -not just passenger cars.
